Here's the thing — it doesn't have to go that way.

A pre-listing inspection means you find out what's going on with your house before anyone else does. You get the full picture — roof, foundation, HVAC, plumbing, electrical, all of it — and then you decide what to do with that information.

It completely changes the dynamic. Instead of being on your heels during negotiation, you're walking in with nothing to hide and everything documented.

Find issues before buyers do — no surprises at the table

2

3

Faster close — less back-and-forth on repair credits
